Guess Hue?

A colorful twist on the classic game of "I Spy". A player is shown a color then given 30 seconds to take a picture of something with that color in it. They are then assigned a score out of 1000 based on how close the picture is to the goal color. Players have a total of 3 rounds, then a final score is recorded.

I designed and implemented this with four other Rice students for Hack.Rice. We placed 3rd overall for the hack-a-thon.

My team an I decided to implement the app in Android since none of us had experience with it before and wanted a learning experience. The photo processing uses an M-tree (with the goal color as a root) to score the photo. Each pixel in the photo is converted into a point in RGB space and placed into the tree. We then query the tree for the closest 1% of pixels, average the color value, and use the distance between the goal color and this average for the score. This allowed us to only grab the intended part of the photo and ignore the background and other noise.
